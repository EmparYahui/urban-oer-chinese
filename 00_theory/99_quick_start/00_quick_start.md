# 🚀 Quick Start Guide  
## Urban Linguistic Landscape OER (A1–A2 Chinese)

---

## 🎯 Purpose

This guide shows how to use this OER in practice in less than 10 minutes, without reading the theoretical framework.

---

## 👤 Who is this for?

### 👩‍🏫 Teachers
You want ready-to-use task sequences.

### 🧑‍🎓 Students
You want to learn Chinese through real urban language.

### 🧠 Designers / Researchers
You want to adapt or reuse the system structure.

---

## 🔁 Core learning cycle (minimum version)

1. **Observe** urban language in real space  
2. **Mediate** meaning (interpret / translate / explain)  
3. **Produce** a multimodal output  

---

## 🧪 Minimal example task (A1 level)

### Step 1 — Observe
Find a Chinese sign in the city (restaurant, shop, transport).

### Step 2 — Capture
Take a photo of the sign.

### Step 3 — Mediate
Answer:
- What does it mean?
- What language elements do you recognize?

### Step 4 — Produce
Create:
- 2–3 sentences in Chinese OR
- 1 bilingual caption (Chinese + your language)

---

## 🧭 AR extension (optional)

If AR tools are available:

- Add a digital note to the sign
- Include translation or explanation
- Attach your interpretation to the location

---

## 📦 Expected output

Each task produces:

- 1 multimodal artefact (text + image or annotation)
- 1 interpreted urban linguistic item
- 1 reusable learning resource

## ⚠️ Important rule

You do NOT need to read the theory first.

Start with a task. Learn by doing. Return to theory later if needed.

---

## 🔄 System logic (simple version)

Urban space → Interpretation → Production → Reuse
