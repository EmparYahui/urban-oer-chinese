# 04 Augmented Reality (AR) as a Mediational Layer in Urban OER Systems

## 1. Conceptual definition

Augmented Reality (AR) is defined in this system as a mediational layer that overlays digital information onto physical urban environments.

AR does not function as an isolated technology but as an extension of multimodal and linguistic mediation processes within situated learning contexts.

---

## 2. AR within the system architecture

AR operates as a bridge between:

- Urban Linguistic Landscapes (physical input)
- Multimodal mediation processes (cognitive transformation)
- OER artefact production (digital output)

It connects space, meaning, and learning.

---

## 3. AR as spatial mediation

AR enables the transformation of urban space into an interactive learning environment by:

- attaching digital annotations to physical signs
- contextualising linguistic input in situ
- linking language data to geographic locations
- enabling spatialised interpretation of meaning

Urban space becomes a layered semiotic system.

---

## 4. Pedagogical role of AR

AR supports learning by:

- situating language within real-world contexts
- enhancing attention to linguistic detail in the environment
- enabling embodied interaction with language
- connecting abstract linguistic concepts to physical experience

Learning becomes spatially anchored.

---

## 5. AR and task-based learning integration

Within the Task + Project-based system, AR is embedded into tasks:

### Observation tasks
- capturing geo-referenced linguistic input
- identifying signs in physical space

### Interpretation tasks
- contextualising meaning using spatial cues
- decoding urban semiotics in situ

### Mediation tasks
- linking digital explanations to physical artefacts
- adding interpretive layers to urban signs

### Production tasks
- creating AR-enhanced multimodal artefacts
- embedding educational content in spatial contexts

---

## 6. AR as extension of multimodality

AR expands multimodality by adding a **spatial-digital layer**.

It integrates:

- linguistic mode (text, translation)
- visual mode (images, overlays)
- spatial mode (location, geography)
- digital mode (interactive content, annotations)

Meaning becomes distributed across space and interface.

---

## 7. AR and learner engagement

AR transforms learners into:

- active explorers of urban environments
- designers of spatialised knowledge
- interpreters of situated linguistic data
- creators of location-based educational resources

Learning becomes embodied and exploratory.

---

## 8. AR and ethnographic methodology

AR supports ethnographic practices by:

- documenting urban linguistic phenomena in context
- enabling situated observation of language use
- linking field data with interpretive layers
- supporting reflective analysis of urban meaning

This aligns with urban walking methodologies and situated inquiry approaches.

---

## 9. AR and OER production

AR-enhanced outputs contribute to OER creation by:

- embedding learning materials in spatial contexts
- generating reusable multimodal artefacts
- documenting situated linguistic environments
- enabling adaptation of location-based learning resources

The city becomes a publishable learning platform.

---

## 10. Cognitive implications of AR

AR supports learning through:

- enhanced situational awareness
- improved memory through spatial association
- integration of visual, linguistic, and spatial cognition
- reinforcement of meaning through context-dependent exposure

Learning is anchored in lived space.

---

## 11. Technical neutrality principle

In this system, AR is considered tool-agnostic.

Any technology capable of:

- spatial annotation
- digital overlay
- contextual information delivery

can function as AR infrastructure within the model.

---

## 12. Connection to other layers

This module connects directly to:

- Layer 1: Urban Linguistic Landscapes → physical input environment
- Layer 2: Task + Project-Based Learning → pedagogical activation
- Layer 3: Multimodality and mediation → cognitive transformation
- Layer 5–6: OER production → final output layer

---

## 13. Final synthesis

Augmented Reality functions as a **mediational spatial layer** that:

- extends urban space into a learning environment
- connects physical and digital meaning systems
- enhances multimodal mediation processes
- enables situated, embodied language learning

AR transforms the city into an interactive, layered pedagogical system.
