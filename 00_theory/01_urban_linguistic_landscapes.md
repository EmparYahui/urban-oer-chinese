# 01 Urban Linguistic Landscapes (ULL) in Task-Based OER Design

## 1. Conceptual definition

Urban Linguistic Landscapes (ULL) refer to the visible and multimodal presence of language in public urban spaces, including signs, advertisements, notices, graffiti, digital displays, and other semiotic artefacts embedded in the city.

In this system, ULL is not treated as a descriptive object but as a pedagogical infrastructure for language learning.

---

## 2. ULL as a learning environment

Within this OER system, urban linguistic landscapes function as:

- authentic sources of linguistic input
- multimodal learning stimuli
- contextual anchors for meaning-making
- triggers for task-based interaction
- sites of linguistic mediation and interpretation

The city is understood as a dynamic and constantly evolving text.

---

## 3. Pedagogical role of ULL

ULL is not an optional resource but a structural component of the learning design.

It enables:

- situated language learning
- contextualised vocabulary acquisition
- real-world interaction with linguistic input
- awareness of multilingual and multicultural environments
- integration of formal and informal language varieties

---

## 4. ULL and task-based learning

In this system, ULL is operationalised through tasks.

Learners engage with urban linguistic data through:

- observation tasks (identifying signs and meanings)
- documentation tasks (capturing and recording input)
- interpretation tasks (translating and mediating meaning)
- transformation tasks (reconstructing input into new artefacts)

Thus, ULL functions as the input layer for Task-Based Language Teaching (TBLT).

---

## 5. ULL as multimodal input

Urban linguistic landscapes are inherently multimodal.

They combine:

- written language
- visual design
- spatial positioning
- cultural references
- digital augmentation (in some cases)

This multimodality is central to meaning construction in the system.

---

## 6. ULL and linguistic mediation

ULL activates mediation processes between:

- languages (e.g., L1 ↔ L2)
- cultural frameworks
- semiotic systems
- institutional and informal discourse

Learners are required to interpret, reformulate, and transform meaning rather than simply decode it.

---

## 7. ULL and augmented reality (AR)

Urban linguistic landscapes can be extended through augmented reality.

AR enables:

- spatial annotation of linguistic elements
- contextual augmentation of meaning
- layering of digital information over physical space
- creation of interactive learning environments

This transforms static urban input into dynamic learning experiences.

---

## 8. ULL in the OER lifecycle

Within the OER system, ULL contributes to:

- generation of learning input
- creation of learner artefacts
- production of reusable educational resources
- documentation of empirical learning processes

Urban input becomes both learning material and research data.

---

## 9. Structural function in the system

ULL operates as the foundational input layer for the entire architecture.

It connects directly with:

- Task-Based Learning (Layer 2)
- Multimodality and mediation (Layer 3)
- AR integration (Layer 4)
- Open Educational Resources (Layer 5–6)

---

## 10. Final synthesis

Urban Linguistic Landscapes are not external context.

They are an active pedagogical system in which:

- the city functions as a text
- language becomes spatially embedded meaning
- learners become interpreters and producers of urban discourse
- tasks emerge from real-world linguistic environments
