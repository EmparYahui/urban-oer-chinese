# 05 Open Educational Resources (OER) and Open Pedagogy in Urban Learning Systems

## 1. Conceptual definition

Open Educational Resources (OER) are defined in this system as educational materials that are freely accessible, adaptable, and reusable, produced through pedagogical processes that embed openness as a structural principle rather than a final licensing step.

Open pedagogy refers to the instructional approach in which learners actively contribute to the creation, adaptation, and dissemination of knowledge resources.

---

## 2. OER as system architecture, not output

In this model, OER is not the final stage of learning but a structural condition embedded across all layers:

- Urban Linguistic Landscapes → source material for open learning input
- Task + Project-Based Learning → generation of open artefacts
- Multimodality and mediation → transformation of knowledge into reusable forms
- AR layer → contextualisation of open knowledge in space
- OER layer → formalisation and dissemination of outputs

Openness is distributed across the entire system.

---

## 3. Open pedagogy principle

Learners are positioned as co-authors of educational resources.

This implies:

- learning outputs are not disposable exercises
- learner productions are treated as publishable artefacts
- knowledge creation is collaborative and iterative
- authorship is distributed between learners and system design

---

## 4. OER production cycle

The system follows a structured cycle:

1. Urban input collection (ULL)
2. Task-based interpretation and mediation
3. Multimodal artefact creation
4. AR contextualisation of outputs
5. Evaluation of pedagogical and linguistic quality
6. Selection of artefacts for open publication
7. Release, reuse, and adaptation

This cycle is recursive and continuously evolving.

---

## 5. Criteria for OER quality

Not all outputs are automatically considered OER.

Artefacts are evaluated according to:

- clarity of linguistic communication
- quality of mediation processes
- multimodal coherence
- pedagogical reusability
- adaptability across contexts and languages
- ethical and anonymisation standards

---

## 6. Learner-generated knowledge

Learners contribute to OER production by generating:

- multimodal urban interpretations
- mediated linguistic transformations
- contextualised explanations of urban signs
- AR-linked spatial artefacts

These outputs function simultaneously as:

- learning outcomes
- research data
- reusable educational materials

---

## 7. Ethical and authorship considerations

Open pedagogy requires explicit attention to:

- informed consent for publication of learner work
- anonymisation of personal data
- recognition of learner authorship
- fair attribution in derivative works
- responsible reuse of urban data

---

## 8. OER and research integration

This system integrates OER production with empirical research through:

- multimodal artefact analysis
- observation of learning processes
- documentation of mediation strategies
- evaluation of pedagogical effectiveness

OER outputs serve as both educational and research artefacts.

---

## 9. Reusability and adaptation principle

All materials are designed for:

- cross-linguistic adaptation
- contextual reconfiguration
- pedagogical reuse in different settings
- scalability across proficiency levels

The system prioritises structural transferability over content fixation.

---

## 10. Open dissemination logic

OER outputs are intended for:

- educational repositories (e.g. GitHub)
- institutional teaching reuse
- research dissemination
- collaborative adaptation by educators and designers

Knowledge circulation is a core design objective.

---

## 11. Connection to system layers

This module connects to:

- Layer 1: Urban input → source of raw material
- Layer 2: Tasks and projects → generation mechanism
- Layer 3: Multimodality and mediation → transformation process
- Layer 4: AR → spatial contextualisation
- Layer 6: Integrated model → system synthesis

---

## 12. Final synthesis

Open Educational Resources in this system are not end products.

They are:

- emergent outputs of pedagogical processes
- co-constructed knowledge artefacts
- structurally open and adaptable materials
- bridges between education, research, and public knowledge

Openness is not added. It is designed.
