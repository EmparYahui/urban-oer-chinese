# 🧠 AR Framework — Augmented Reality as Mediational Design

## 🧩 Overview

This document defines the conceptual role of **Augmented Reality (AR)** in the OER system.

AR is understood as a **mediational layer that connects physical urban space with digital multimodal learning artefacts**.

---

## 🧠 Conceptual definition

AR is:

> a design principle that enables the spatial recontextualisation of learner-generated meaning

It connects:

- urban linguistic landscapes
- learner interpretation and mediation
- multimodal digital artefacts
- situated learning processes

---

## 🎯 Pedagogical role

AR supports learning through three mechanisms:

### 1. Spatial mediation
Meaning is anchored in physical urban locations.

### 2. Recontextualisation
Learner artefacts gain meaning in situ.

### 3. Open circulation
Outputs become reusable learning resources beyond the classroom.

---

## 🧠 Key principle

AR is not a tool.

It is a **way of designing learning that extends meaning into space**.

---

## 🔗 System integration

AR connects directly with:

- Learning Tasks (02 module)
- Assessment system (03 module)
- Multimodal production processes

---

## Notes for implementation

- AR complexity can be adapted depending on institutional resources
- Low-tech alternatives (QR codes, maps) are fully compatible
- The pedagogical model remains valid independent of platform choice
