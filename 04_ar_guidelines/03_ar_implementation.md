# AR Guidelines — Implementing AR in Learning Tasks

## 🧠 Overview

This document provides detailed guidance on how to incorporate **Augmented Reality (AR)** into the **learning tasks** of the Urban Linguistic Landscape OER system. These tasks leverage AR to facilitate a deeper engagement with the urban linguistic environment, enabling learners to actively explore, interpret, and mediate linguistic and cultural content.

---

## 🧩 How AR Fits Into Learning Tasks

### AR in Urban Observation Tasks

- **Objective**: Learners use AR tools to explore real urban signage and semiotic elements, annotating them with linguistic and cultural information.
- **Example**: Learners scan QR codes placed in the city that trigger AR overlays, providing translations, historical context, and cultural significance of the urban signs.
  
### AR in Mediation and Transformation Tasks

- **Objective**: Learners use AR to mediate and adapt the meanings of urban signs or phrases, translating them into Chinese or another target language.
- **Example**: Learners capture photos of urban signs, and the AR application enables them to overlay the text with their own translations and cultural interpretations.

### AR in Multimodal Production Tasks

- **Objective**: Learners create multimedia artefacts that integrate AR, text, images, and voice.
- **Example**: Learners produce an AR-enhanced tour of a city, where they provide commentary on the urban landscape, using their smartphone or AR glasses.

---

## 🎯 Best Practices for Implementing AR

To maximize the effectiveness of AR in your learning tasks, consider the following best practices:

1. **User-Friendly Tools**: Choose AR tools that are easy to use for learners, ensuring smooth interactions.
2. **Contextual Relevance**: Ensure that AR experiences are linked to the linguistic and cultural elements that learners are studying.
3. **Task Design**: Focus on tasks that encourage meaningful interactions, where learners critically engage with both the AR content and the physical environment.
