# 04 AR Guidelines — Augmented Reality in Urban Linguistic Landscape Learning

## Overview

This module defines the role of Augmented Reality (AR) within the Urban Linguistic Landscape OER intervention for beginner Chinese (A1–A2).

AR is conceptualised not as a technological add-on, but as a **contextual mediation layer** that connects digital artefacts with physical urban environments.

It operates as part of an integrated pedagogical system combining:
- linguistic mediation
- multimodal production
- urban situated learning
- Open Educational Resources (OER)

---

## Conceptual foundation

In this OER, AR is understood as:

> a mediational infrastructure that enables the spatial recontextualisation of learner-generated artefacts

Rather than functioning as an isolated tool, AR supports meaning-making by linking:

- physical urban spaces (linguistic landscapes)
- digital multimodal artefacts
- learner interpretation and mediation processes

This aligns with research on situated learning, multimodality, and digital mediation in language education.

---

## Pedagogical role of AR

AR supports the intervention in three interconnected ways:

### 1. Spatial mediation
AR connects linguistic artefacts to real-world locations, allowing learners to anchor meaning in context.

### 2. Recontextualisation of meaning
Learner-produced artefacts are reinterpreted within physical space, reinforcing mediation processes.

### 3. Extension of OER accessibility
Geolocated artefacts become reusable learning resources accessible beyond the classroom.

---

## Integration within the learning design

AR is embedded in Phase 3 of the learning sequence:

### Step 1 — Artefact creation
Learners produce multimodal artefacts based on urban linguistic input.

### Step 2 — Geolocation
Artefacts are associated with specific urban locations relevant to their meaning.

### Step 3 — AR publication
Artefacts are uploaded to AR platforms or geolocation tools for mobile access.

### Step 4 — Situated interaction
Learners (and external users) access artefacts in context, linking digital and physical environments.

---

## Tools and implementation options

The framework is tool-agnostic. Possible implementations include:

- mobile AR applications
- geolocation-based mapping tools
- institutional AR platforms
- simple QR-code linking systems

The choice of tool does not affect the pedagogical model, as long as spatial linkage is maintained.

---

## Design principles

All AR integration must follow these principles:

- **Contextual relevance**: AR content must be meaningfully linked to location  
- **Pedagogical clarity**: artefacts must remain understandable without technical mediation  
- **Accessibility**: resources should be usable on standard mobile devices  
- **Reusability**: AR-linked artefacts should function as OER  
- **Minimal technological dependency**: pedagogy is not tool-dependent

## Key conceptual insight

AR is not a tool used in the learning process.

It is a **design principle that extends learning into physical space**, enabling:

- situated meaning-making
- multimodal recontextualisation
- open educational circulation of artefacts

---

## Notes for implementation

- AR complexity can be adapted depending on institutional resources
- Low-tech alternatives (QR codes, maps) are fully compatible
- The pedagogical model remains valid independent of platform choice
