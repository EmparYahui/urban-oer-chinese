# 05 AR Guidelines — Exemplars of AR Task Designs

## 🧠 Overview

This document provides concrete examples of **Augmented Reality (AR)** tasks designed to enhance language learning through interaction with urban linguistic landscapes. These exemplars show how AR can be used effectively within the OER framework.

---

## 🧩 AR Task Design Examples

### 1. **Urban Sign Exploration Task**
- **Objective**: Learners scan real-world urban signs to identify linguistic elements and cultural context, overlaying AR content for further information.
- **Tools**: ARCore/ARKit, QR code scanning.
- **Steps**:
  1. Students scan QR codes attached to city signs.
  2. AR overlays show the translation of signs and offer cultural explanations in the target language.
  3. Learners annotate their observations and reflect on the urban space in a collaborative digital artefact.

### 2. **Interactive City Tour**
- **Objective**: Learners create an AR-enhanced city tour, annotating key locations and signs with information in Chinese.
- **Tools**: Unity, ARCore/ARKit, mobile device.
- **Steps**:
  1. Students create AR markers at various locations.
  2. Learners record information about each location, integrating photos, text, and audio in Chinese.
  3. The final product is a navigable AR city tour that can be shared with others.

---

## 🎯 Tips for Designing AR Tasks

1. **Focus on Real-World Context**: Ensure that tasks are connected to the learners' physical environment, reinforcing the learning objectives.
2. **Encourage Multimodal Outputs**: Students should integrate various media forms—text, images, and audio—into their AR projects to enhance language acquisition and production.
3. **Simplicity**: AR tasks should be straightforward to execute, with clear instructions and intuitive interfaces.
