# 02 AR Guidelines — Overview

## 🧠 Overview

This document provides a comprehensive introduction to the integration of **Augmented Reality (AR)** in the Urban Linguistic Landscape OER system. AR serves as a mediating tool that connects the physical and digital spaces, enhancing learners' engagement with linguistic and semiotic elements in the urban environment.

AR is a key feature in facilitating multimodal learning by allowing students to interact with both real-world and virtual content. This interaction enriches the learning experience, making it more engaging and contextually grounded.

---

## 🎯 Purpose of AR in the OER

The main objective of integrating AR is to enhance the learning experience by:

- **Contextualizing urban linguistic landscapes**: AR helps learners understand and interact with real-world language data in situ.
- **Mediating meaning**: AR bridges the gap between linguistic and cultural information, allowing for deeper comprehension and negotiation of meaning.
- **Supporting multimodal production**: AR is a tool for creating dynamic, multimodal outputs that integrate text, images, and sound.
  
AR allows learners to move beyond traditional classroom settings, engaging with real-world data and enhancing their cultural and linguistic competence.

---

## 🧩 AR Integration Strategy

The use of AR is divided into three primary phases of the OER system:

1. **Urban Observation**: Learners engage with their surroundings by identifying linguistic signs and semiotic elements through an AR interface.
2. **Mediation and Transformation**: Learners use AR to reinterpret, adapt, and transform urban signs, applying linguistic and cultural knowledge.
3. **Multimodal Production**: Learners produce digital artefacts (e.g., augmented city tours, annotated images) that combine text, visuals, and audio to represent their understanding.

---

## 🔁 How AR Enhances Learning

AR contributes to the OER system by providing learners with:

- **Interactive engagement**: Immersing learners in real-world data and context.
- **Contextual learning**: Offering a deeper understanding of the cultural, historical, and linguistic background of the urban environment.
- **Multimodal outputs**: Encouraging learners to produce content that combines various media, reinforcing language and cultural learning.
