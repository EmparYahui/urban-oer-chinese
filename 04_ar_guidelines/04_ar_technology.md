# 04 AR Guidelines — Technology Requirements and Tools

## 🧠 Overview

This document outlines the **technical infrastructure** needed to implement **Augmented Reality (AR)** within the Urban Linguistic Landscape OER system. It covers the software and hardware tools required for AR integration, ensuring that teachers and learners can effectively utilize AR as part of the learning process.

---

## 🧰 AR Software Tools

### 1. **ARCore (Android) / ARKit (iOS)**
- Both platforms allow developers to create AR experiences on Android and iOS devices, respectively.
  
### 2. **Unity**
- A popular game engine that can be used to create immersive AR experiences, such as location-based interactions and 3D models in real-world environments.

### 3. **Spark AR Studio**
- A tool for creating augmented reality filters, commonly used for social media platforms but can be repurposed for educational contexts.

---

## 🧰 Hardware Requirements

### 1. **Mobile Devices**
- Most modern smartphones and tablets (with ARCore or ARKit support) are sufficient for the AR tasks in this system.

### 2. **AR Glasses** (Optional)
- Devices like **Microsoft HoloLens** or **Magic Leap** can offer more immersive experiences, though they are not required for basic AR tasks.

---

## 🎯 Best Practices for AR Tools and Technology

- **Compatibility**: Ensure that the chosen AR tools work across devices most commonly used by students.
- **Usability**: Prioritize AR tools that offer simple interfaces and reliable performance to avoid distracting students from the content.
- **Cost Considerations**: While advanced tools like AR glasses may offer immersive experiences, AR can be effectively implemented on smartphones and tablets, reducing barriers to entry.
