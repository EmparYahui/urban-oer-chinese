# Using This Corpus

## Purpose

This guide explains how to use the learner-generated artefacts presented in this module. The corpus has been designed to support three complementary audiences—researchers, teachers, and students—each of whom may interpret and use the examples for different educational purposes.

Rather than providing fixed models to replicate, the corpus encourages evidence-informed analysis, pedagogical adaptation, and reflective learning.

---

# For Researchers

Researchers may use the corpus to examine authentic learner-generated artefacts within the context of task-based, situated, and multimodal language learning.

Possible applications include:

- analysing learner language production;
- studying multimodal communication;
- investigating digital and spatial learning;
- examining collaborative knowledge construction;
- comparing instructional implementations across contexts.

The artefacts complement the findings presented in the accompanying research article by providing direct examples of learner production.

---

# For Teachers

Teachers may use the corpus to understand how the instructional model is implemented in practice.

The examples can support:

- lesson planning;
- task design;
- assessment design;
- classroom discussion;
- teacher professional development;
- adaptation to different educational contexts.

The objective is to inspire informed adaptation rather than procedural replication.

---

# For Students

Students may consult the corpus to better understand the characteristics of authentic communicative tasks and multimodal production.

The examples help learners to:

- understand task expectations;
- explore different ways of combining communicative modes;
- reflect on quality criteria;
- compare alternative solutions;
- develop greater autonomy during project work.

The artefacts are intended to inspire new ideas rather than provide templates for imitation.

---

# Recommendations for Reuse

When using the examples, readers are encouraged to:

- interpret each artefact within its pedagogical context;
- consider the relationship between tasks, assessment, and learner production;
- adapt activities to local educational needs;
- preserve the pedagogical principles while modifying contextual elements;
- acknowledge the original repository when appropriate.

Successful transfer depends on adaptation rather than direct reproduction.

---

# Relationship with the Repository

This corpus should be explored together with the other modules of the repository.

- **01 Teaching Design** explains why the artefacts were produced.
- **02 Learning Tasks** describes how they were created.
- **03 Assessment** explains how learning was evaluated.
- **05 Templates** provides reusable instructional resources.
- **07 Use Cases** demonstrates how the instructional model can be adapted to new contexts.

Together, these modules document the complete instructional cycle from design to pedagogical transfer.

---

# Key Takeaway

This corpus is intended to be used as a resource for analysis, adaptation, and learning rather than as a collection of model answers. Its educational value lies in making authentic learner production visible so that researchers, teachers, and students can better understand, interpret, and adapt the instructional model to their own contexts.
