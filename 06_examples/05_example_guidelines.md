# 05 Example Guidelines — Urban Linguistic Landscape OER

## 🧠 Overview

This document provides guidelines for creating **learner examples** and **model outputs** that align with the **Urban Linguistic Landscape OER** framework. It helps educators and researchers understand the pedagogical principles behind each type of artefact and ensures that examples meet the educational goals.

---

## 🧩 Example Creation Guidelines

### 1. **Urban Sign Mediation**
- **Description**: Students are encouraged to select a real-world urban sign and recontextualize it as a Chinese learning resource.
- **Expected Components**:
  - Image of the urban sign.
  - Chinese translation of the message.
  - Bilingual notes or explanation of cultural elements.
- **Pedagogical Focus**: Vocabulary acquisition, cultural adaptation, and linguistic mediation.

### 2. **Multimodal Infographic**
- **Description**: Learners create infographics that combine text, images, and captions to explain a concept or describe a place.
- **Expected Components**:
  - A visually appealing design integrating text and images.
  - Clear Chinese captions that highlight key vocabulary.
- **Pedagogical Focus**: Multimodal composition, writing skills, and vocabulary reinforcement.

### 3. **AR-Linked Geolocated Artefact**
- **Description**: Artefacts connected to specific urban locations using AR.
- **Expected Components**:
  - Digital artefact (image + text).
  - Geolocation markers or QR codes.
  - AR access link to enrich the urban experience.
- **Pedagogical Focus**: Situated learning, spatial awareness, and use of AR for educational purposes.

---

## 🧩 Purpose

These guidelines help in creating consistent and meaningful examples that can serve as models for learners and provide concrete reference points for assessment.

---

## 🧩 Notes for Adaptation

- Flexible for different proficiency levels and languages.
- Can be applied to a wide variety of urban environments.
