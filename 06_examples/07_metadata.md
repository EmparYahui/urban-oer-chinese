# Metadata

## Purpose

This document describes the provenance, organisation, selection criteria, and ethical considerations associated with the learner-generated artefacts presented in this module.

Providing metadata enhances the transparency, reproducibility, and educational value of the corpus by documenting how the artefacts were created, selected, and prepared for publication.

---

# Corpus Description

The corpus consists of learner-generated artefacts produced during the implementation of the instructional model documented throughout this repository.

The materials include multimodal productions created in response to authentic communicative tasks and may comprise:

- geolocated digital maps;
- written texts;
- photographs;
- audio recordings;
- video materials;
- augmented reality resources;
- collaborative digital documents.

The corpus represents a curated selection rather than the complete set of learner productions.

---

# Selection Criteria

Artefacts were selected according to the following principles:

- pedagogical relevance;
- representation of different learning tasks;
- diversity of multimodal production;
- evidence of learner engagement;
- usefulness for research, teaching, and future learning.

Selection was guided by educational value rather than by linguistic perfection.

---

# Ethical Considerations

All published artefacts have been prepared in accordance with responsible educational and research practices.

The preparation process included:

- anonymisation of learner identities;
- removal of personal information where necessary;
- verification of copyright compliance;
- review of materials before publication.

The published corpus is intended exclusively for educational and research purposes.

---

# Relationship with the Research

The artefacts documented in this repository complement the analyses presented in the accompanying research article.

While the article reports aggregated findings, the corpus provides authentic examples illustrating the learner productions that informed those analyses.

Together, the article and the repository provide complementary forms of evidence supporting the instructional model.

---

# Citation and Reuse

Users are encouraged to cite both the accompanying publication and this repository when referring to the instructional model or its learner-generated artefacts.

When adapting materials, users should:

- acknowledge the original source;
- preserve attribution information;
- indicate substantial modifications where appropriate;
- comply with the repository licence.

Responsible reuse contributes to the continuous development of Open Educational Resources and Open Educational Practices.

# Key Takeaway

The educational value of this corpus depends not only on the learner-generated artefacts themselves but also on the transparency with which they are documented. By describing their provenance, selection, and ethical preparation, this metadata supports responsible interpretation, pedagogical reuse, and future educational research.
