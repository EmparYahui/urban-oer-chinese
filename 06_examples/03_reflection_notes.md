# 03 Reflection Notes — Urban Linguistic Landscape OER

## 🧠 Overview

This document provides reflection prompts and guidelines to help learners critically engage with their work produced within the **Urban Linguistic Landscape OER**. It supports educators in facilitating reflective practices that encourage learners to evaluate their linguistic mediation, multimodal production, and potential for reuse.

This reflection document is intended to be used in conjunction with other OER modules such as:

- **[01 Teaching Design](../01_teaching_design/README.md)**: To connect reflection with overall pedagogical goals.
- **[02 Learning Tasks](../02_learning_tasks/README.md)**: To ensure that reflections are grounded in task-based activities and real-world urban linguistic environments.
- **[03 Assessment](../03_assessment/README.md)**: To align reflective practices with the evaluation criteria used to assess learner artefacts.
- **[04 AR Guidelines](../04_ar_guidelines/README.md)**: To reflect on the use of AR in urban learning experiences.

---

## 🧩 Reflection Prompts

### 1. **On Linguistic Mediation**
- How did you decide on the level of complexity for your translation or adaptation?
- Did you encounter any difficulties in choosing the right words or phrases for your target language? How did you resolve them?

### 2. **On Multimodal Production**
- How did you choose which modes (text, image, audio) to use in your artefact?
- How did the integration of different modes help convey your message? Were there any challenges in balancing them?

### 3. **On AR Integration (If Used)**
- How did you integrate AR into your artefact? What was your reasoning for choosing a particular location or AR tool?
- Did the AR component enhance the learning experience for you? Why or why not?

### 4. **On Reusability and OER Potential**
- Do you believe your artefact could be useful for other learners or in other educational contexts? Why?
- How could your artefact be adapted or improved to make it more reusable?

---

## 🧩 Example Reflection

**Task**: Urban Advertisement Mediation

- **Linguistic Mediation**: I found it challenging to convey the full meaning of the advertisement due to cultural differences. I simplified some phrases but still tried to keep the message clear.
- **Multimodal Production**: I used an image of the advertisement along with Chinese text. The text highlighted key vocabulary, and the image helped give context. However, I think I could have made the text more visible.
- **AR Integration**: I used a QR code linked to an online map of the urban area where the advertisement was located. I think the integration worked, but the QR code should be placed in a more prominent location.
- **Reusability**: The artefact could definitely be reused in other beginner-level Chinese classes. If I had more time, I would add an audio component for pronunciation practice.
