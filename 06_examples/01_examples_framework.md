# Examples Framework

## Purpose

This module presents a curated corpus of learner-generated artefacts produced during the implementation of the instructional model documented throughout this repository. Rather than functioning as a gallery of student work, the corpus provides evidence of how learners engaged with authentic communicative tasks, multimodal production, and situated learning within an urban environment.

The examples are intended to support research, teaching, and learning simultaneously. They document observable learning outcomes, illustrate the implementation of the instructional design, and provide reference artefacts that can inform future educational practice.

---

# What This Corpus Contains

The corpus includes a representative selection of anonymised learner-generated artefacts created throughout the instructional sequence.

These materials include:

- geolocated digital maps;
- multimodal productions combining text, image, audio, and video;
- augmented reality resources;
- reflective learning products;
- examples illustrating different stages of task completion and revision.

The artefacts were selected to represent the diversity of learning outcomes generated during the project rather than to showcase only exemplary work.

---

# Selection Principles

The examples included in this corpus were selected according to the following criteria:

- relevance to the instructional model;
- representation of different phases of the learning process;
- diversity of multimodal productions;
- pedagogical value for future implementation;
- potential for adaptation and reuse as Open Educational Resources (OERs).

All learner materials have been anonymised before publication.

---

# How to Read the Examples

Each example can be interpreted from three complementary perspectives.

## Research Perspective

The artefacts constitute empirical evidence of learner production and document observable aspects of language development, multimodal communication, collaboration, and situated learning. They complement the analyses reported in the accompanying research article by providing authentic examples of learner-generated data.

## Teaching Perspective

The examples illustrate how the instructional model operates in practice. Rather than prescribing fixed classroom procedures, they demonstrate how authentic tasks, digital technologies, assessment strategies, and multimodal production can be integrated into coherent learning experiences that may be adapted to different educational contexts.

## Student Perspective

The artefacts provide transparent examples of expected learning outcomes. They help learners understand the nature of multimodal production, the characteristics of authentic communicative tasks, and the quality standards expected throughout the instructional sequence. They are intended to inspire new productions rather than to serve as models for direct imitation.

---

# Relationship with the Repository

This corpus represents the practical implementation of the instructional model described throughout the repository.

Its connections with the other modules are as follows:

- **00 Theory** provides the conceptual foundations underpinning the instructional model.
- **01 Teaching Design** explains the pedagogical architecture that guided the intervention.
- **02 Learning Tasks** describes the activities through which the artefacts were produced.
- **03 Assessment** documents the evaluation framework applied during the learning process.
- **04 AR Guidelines** explains the role of spatial mediation and augmented reality.
- **05 Templates** provides reusable resources supporting learner production.
- **07 Use Cases** demonstrates how the instructional model and its artefacts can be transferred to new educational contexts.

Together, these modules document the complete cycle from instructional design to learner production and pedagogical transfer.

---

# Key Takeaway

The examples presented in this module are not intended as model answers or isolated classroom products. They constitute a documented corpus of learner-generated artefacts that provides empirical evidence of the instructional model in action while supporting pedagogical adaptation, professional development, and future research in second language education.
