# Introduction

Traditional task-based language teaching (TBLT) has been widely adopted in second language education due to its emphasis on meaningful communication and learner-centred task design. However, its implementation often remains constrained by classroom-bound contexts, limiting opportunities for situated, experiential, and multimodal language use.

In response to these limitations, this paper introduces the Urban Linguistic Landscape OER, a pedagogical system that reconfigures urban environments as sites for language learning through mediation, multimodal production, and open educational resource creation. The framework positions learners not only as task performers but as producers of reusable educational artefacts embedded in real-world linguistic ecologies.

Drawing on linguistic landscape studies, multimodal pedagogy, translanguaging theory, and design-based research, the project integrates structured learning tasks with mediation-oriented activities that transform urban input into multilingual outputs. These outputs are further developed into open educational resources (OER), enabling reuse, adaptation, and redistribution across contexts.

The system also incorporates optional augmented reality (AR) and geolocation components, extending learning beyond the classroom into spatially situated environments. This integration supports embodied interaction with language in public spaces, enhancing both cognitive and experiential dimensions of learning.

By combining task-based learning with open educational practices, the framework proposes a shift from task completion toward knowledge production, positioning language learning as a process of mediated, multimodal, and open-ended meaning-making.
