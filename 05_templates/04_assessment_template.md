# 04 Assessment Template — Urban Linguistic Landscape OER

## 🧠 Overview

This template provides a **standardized framework** for designing assessments that align with the principles of the **Urban Linguistic Landscape OER**. It is designed to evaluate **learner artefacts** produced within a **task-based, multimodal learning environment**, with a particular focus on **linguistic mediation** and **urban linguistic landscapes**. This tool ensures that evaluations are consistent, transparent, and adaptable across contexts.

---

## 🧩 Template Structure

1: Initial; 2: Basic; 3: Intermediate; 4: Advanced

| **Assessment Field**         | **Description**                                                                                      |
|------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Assessment Title**          | Title of the assessment task (e.g., "Urban Advertisement Mediation").                                |
| **Assessment Objective**      | Clear description of what the assessment is designed to evaluate (e.g., "Evaluate the student’s ability to mediate meaning across languages").                  |
| **Assessment Dimensions**     | List of dimensions evaluated in the task (e.g., Linguistic Clarity, Mediation Quality, Multimodal Production, OER Potential).                                |
| **Grading Rubric**            | Reference to the rubrics used for evaluation (→ [Rubrics](../02_rubrics_multimodal.md)).             |
| **Reflection Prompts**        | Prompts to encourage students' self-reflection (e.g., "How did you adapt the message for a different audience?").                                               |

## 🧩 Assessment Dimensions

### 1. **Linguistic Clarity**

| **Level**                       | **Descriptor**                                                                                     |
|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1**                  | Frequent errors hinder comprehension; message is unclear.                                           |
| **2**                    | Errors present but overall intelligibility maintained. Some areas may be confusing or unclear.      |
| **3**             | Generally accurate with minor errors that do not hinder comprehension. Message is clear and appropriate. |
| **4**                 | High accuracy, coherence, and pragmatic adequacy. Language is precise, clear, and highly effective.   |

---

### 2. **Mediation Quality**

| **Level**                       | **Descriptor**                                                                                     |
|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1**                  | Literal reproduction without adaptation or interpretation.                                          |
| **2**                    | Limited adaptation with minimal contextualisation. Message is slightly reinterpreted but may lack cultural sensitivity. |
| **3**             | Appropriate reformulation with some cultural interpretation. Contextual understanding is evident but could be enhanced. |
| **4**                 | Effective mediation with contextual adaptation and negotiation of meaning. Strong cultural sensitivity and meaningful transformation across languages. |

---

### 3. **Multimodal Production**

| **Level**                       | **Descriptor**                                                                                     |
|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1**                  | Limited use of modes (text, image, audio), lack of coherence; multimodal elements are disconnected or poorly integrated. |
| **2**                    | Basic integration of modes with partial coherence. The elements are somewhat related but could be further developed or connected. |
| **3**             | Coherent multimodal integration, where text, image, and/or audio are appropriately used and contribute to the overall meaning. |
| **4**                 | Rich and coherent integration with strategic use of resources. Elements are seamlessly linked to form a clear and effective narrative. |

---

### 4. **OER Potential**

| **Level**                       | **Descriptor**                                                                                     |
|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1**                  | Difficult to reuse; lacks structure and clarity. Needs major adaptation.                           |
| **2**                    | Limited reusability; requires significant adaptation or restructuring to be usable in other contexts. |
| **3**             | Reusable with minor adaptations. The artefact is clear and structured but may need slight adjustments for broader use. |
| **4**                 | Highly reusable, adaptable, and pedagogically clear. Can be directly applied in other contexts without significant changes. |

---

### 5. **Reflective Quality**

| **Level**                       | **Descriptor**                                                                                     |
|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1**                  | Reflection is basic, covering only surface-level aspects of the task. No deep analysis of the mediation process. |
| **2**                    | Some reflective thinking, but the analysis is shallow. Mentions challenges, but lacks details on how the task was adapted or interpreted. |
| **3**             | Reflection is thoughtful, mentioning challenges and strategies for mediation. Some analysis of how language and meaning were transformed. |
| **4**                 | Deep reflection with critical insights into the mediation process. Clear discussion on how language, culture, and meaning were adapted. |

---

## 🧩 Purpose of the Template

This template is designed to:

- **Ensure consistency**: By aligning assessment tasks with the overarching pedagogical system.
- **Support teachers**: By providing a clear and structured framework for evaluating learner outputs.
- **Foster reusability**: The template helps create assessments that can be easily adapted and reused in various contexts and educational settings.
- **Encourage self-assessment**: With reflective prompts that encourage students to critically evaluate their learning process.

---

## 🧩 Example of Task Assessment

| **Task Title**         | **Urban Advertisement Mediation**                                                                                           |
|------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Objective**           | Evaluate the student’s ability to mediate an urban advertisement (in Spanish) to Chinese, focusing on both linguistic clarity and cultural adaptation. |
| **Assessment Dimensions**| Linguistic Clarity, Mediation Quality, Multimodal Production, OER Potential, Reflective Quality                               |
| **Grading Rubric**      | Level 1: **Limited mediation**, with errors in both language and cultural adaptation. <br> Level 2: **Basic mediation**, with minimal cultural adaptation. Language is understandable but contains several errors. <br> Level 3: **Appropriate mediation**, with effective language use and minor cultural adjustments. <br> Level 4: **Advanced mediation**, with accurate, culturally-sensitive language use and clear adaptation to the target audience. |
| **Reflection Prompts**  | - What challenges did you face in adapting the advertisement? <br> - How did you ensure cultural relevance in the Chinese version? <br> - What would you change if you had to adapt this advertisement again? |

## 🧩 Integration with OER

This assessment template is designed to integrate seamlessly with the other modules in the Urban Linguistic Landscape OER system:

- **01 Teaching Design**: Aligns assessment tasks with pedagogical planning and learning goals.  
- **02 Learning Tasks**: Provides specific tasks for students that are evaluated using this template.  
- **03 Assessment**: Standardizes the evaluation process for multimodal artefacts.  
- **04 AR Guidelines**: Incorporates AR-related dimensions into task-based assessments where relevant.  
- **05 Templates**: Provides scaffolds for designing assessments and tasks that use the evaluation criteria.

---

## 🧩 Notes for Adaptation

- The templates can be simplified or expanded depending on the **student level** and **context**. For example, higher-level students may require more complex tasks that involve deeper cultural and linguistic mediation.
- These templates are **language-independent** and can be adapted for use with any target language.
- The **AR integration** is optional but can be used to link learning tasks with real-world urban environments.
