# 05 Task to AR Integration — Spatial Mediation Layer

## 🧠 Overview

This document defines how learning tasks are extended into Augmented Reality (AR) environments within the Urban Linguistic Landscape OER system.

It operationalises spatial mediation as part of task execution.

---

## 🌍 Conceptual foundation

AR is treated as a **mediational layer**, not a standalone technology.

It connects:

- urban physical space  
- linguistic meaning  
- multimodal artefacts  
- learner interpretation  

---

## 🧭 When AR is used

Tasks are extended into AR when:

- meaning depends on spatial context  
- urban environment is part of interpretation  
- multimodal output benefits from location anchoring  
- learners need situated reinforcement of meaning  

---

## 🧩 AR integration modes

### 1. Annotation mode
Adding digital information to physical urban elements.

- labels
- translations
- explanations
- interpretive notes

---

### 2. Contextualisation mode
Linking meaning to specific places or environments.

- location-based interpretation
- cultural framing of urban signs
- situational meaning activation

---

### 3. Interaction mode
Learners engage with AR layers to reinterpret input.

- scanning / viewing AR content
- comparing interpretations
- revising meaning based on spatial cues

---

## 🔗 Task mapping to AR

### Observational tasks → AR annotation
- capturing urban input
- attaching descriptive layers

### Mediation tasks → AR contextualisation
- linking meaning across languages and contexts
- embedding interpretation in space

### Production tasks → AR embedding
- placing multimodal artefacts in physical space
- connecting output to location

---

## 🧠 System role

This module functions as the **spatial extension layer** of the system.

It transforms learning from:

> text-based interpretation → situated spatial experience

---

## 📦 Outputs

- AR-linked learning artefacts  
- spatial task workflows  
- location-based mediation structures  
- augmented multimodal outputs
