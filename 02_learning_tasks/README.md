# Learning Tasks — Urban Linguistic Landscape OER (A1–A2 Chinese)

## Overview

This section contains task sequences designed for beginner-level Chinese (A1–A2 CEFR) within urban linguistic landscape environments.

The tasks are grounded in Task-Based Language Teaching (TBLT) and designed to promote multimodal production, linguistic mediation, and open educational reuse.

---

## Task design principles

All tasks follow these principles:

- Authenticity: based on real urban linguistic landscapes  
- Mediation: learners interpret and transform meaning across languages  
- Multimodality: integration of text, image, and optional audio/video  
- Reusability: outputs are designed as potential OER artefacts  
- Openness: tasks encourage sharing and adaptation  

---

## Task sequence

### Task 1 — Urban text collection
Learners identify and document linguistic landscape elements (signs, shop names, advertisements, public messages) in their urban environment.

**Output:** photo + short descriptive annotation (L1 or L2)

---

### Task 2 — Meaning mediation
Learners select one item from Task 1 and mediate its meaning into Chinese (A1–A2 level), considering audience and communicative purpose.

**Output:** bilingual caption (L1 → Chinese)

---

### Task 3 — Multimodal re-design
Learners transform the selected urban item into a learning artefact by combining image and text.

**Output:** annotated visual artefact (digital format)

---

### Task 4 — Contextual reinterpretation
Learners reinterpret the same item for a different communicative context (e.g., tourist guide, language learner, cultural explanation).

**Output:** short multimodal adaptation

---

### Task 5 — AR linking (optional)
Learners associate their artefact with a physical location using AR tools or geolocated platforms.

**Output:** AR-enabled or location-linked artefact

---

## Assessment focus

Evaluation is based on:

- communicative effectiveness  
- quality of mediation between languages  
- multimodal integration  
- appropriateness for reuse as OER  
- engagement with urban context  

---

## Notes for reuse

These tasks can be:

- adapted for other target languages  
- simplified or expanded depending on proficiency level  
- used independently or as a sequence  
- integrated into project-based or CLIL contexts
