# Learning Tasks — Urban Linguistic Landscape OER (A1–A2 Chinese)

## Overview

This module contains task sequences designed for beginner-level Chinese (A1–A2 CEFR) within urban linguistic landscape environments.

The tasks are grounded in Task-Based Language Teaching (TBLT) and an open pedagogy framework in which learner outputs are designed as potential Open Educational Resources (OER).

Urban environments, linguistic mediation, multimodal production, and augmented reality (AR) function as integrated components of a single instructional system.

---

## Pedagogical principles

All tasks follow the following principles:

- **Authenticity**: tasks are based on real urban linguistic landscapes  
- **Mediation**: learners interpret, transform, and recontextualise meaning across languages  
- **Multimodality**: integration of text, image, and optional audio/video  
- **Situated learning**: learning is embedded in real urban environments  
- **Reusability (OER principle)**: outputs are designed for adaptation and reuse  
- **Openness as design**: clarity, audience awareness, and transferability are embedded in task design  
- **Technological mediation (AR)**: AR is used to link digital artefacts with physical urban spaces  

---

## Task sequence aligned with research design

The tasks correspond to the three phases of the intervention described in the study.

---

## Phase 1 — Urban observation and linguistic awareness

### Task 1 — Urban linguistic landscape mapping

Learners identify and document linguistic elements in urban spaces (signs, advertisements, shop names, graffiti).

**Output:**
- annotated photograph
- brief descriptive note (L1 or supported L2)

**Purpose:**
To develop awareness of urban spaces as semiotic and pedagogical environments.

---

### Task 2 — Selection and interpretation

Learners select one linguistic item and interpret its meaning, function, and context.

**Output:**
- justification of selection
- initial interpretation of meaning and communicative function

**Purpose:**
To activate interpretive mediation and prepare for transformation.

---

## Phase 2 — Linguistic mediation and multimodal production

### Task 3 — Meaning mediation into Chinese

Learners reformulate the selected urban element into simplified Chinese (A1–A2), adapting meaning for communicative clarity and audience.

**Output:**
- short Chinese text
- optional bilingual support notes

**Purpose:**
To develop linguistic and intercultural mediation strategies.

---

### Task 4 — Multimodal artefact creation

Learners transform mediated content into a multimodal artefact combining:

- Chinese text  
- image(s)  
- optional audio/video  

**Output:**
- infographic / annotated image / digital poster

**Purpose:**
To consolidate meaning through multimodal production and hybrid writing.

---

## Phase 3 — AR integration and recontextualisation

### Task 5 — AR linking and spatial embedding

Learners associate their artefact with a physical urban location using AR tools or geolocated platforms.

**Output:**
- AR entry or geolocated artefact

**Purpose:**
To situate learning in physical space and enable contextualised reuse.

---

### Task 6 — Recontextualised communication (adaptive task)

Learners adapt their artefact for a different communicative context (e.g. tourist guide, language learner support, cultural explanation).

**Output:**
- adapted multimodal version

**Purpose:**
To evaluate transferability and OER potential.

---

## Assessment alignment (link to 03_assessment)

Task performance is evaluated across:

- linguistic and intercultural mediation  
- multimodal coherence  
- linguistic competence (A1–A2 level)  
- OER potential (clarity, reusability, adaptability)  
- engagement with urban and AR-mediated contexts  

---

## Notes for reuse

These tasks can be:

- adapted for other target languages or urban contexts  
- simplified or expanded depending on proficiency level  
- used independently or as a full sequence  
- integrated into CLIL, TBLT, or project-based learning designs  
- implemented with different AR or geolocation tools depending on institutional constraints  

---

## Conceptual alignment

This task sequence operationalises the research design by integrating:

- Urban linguistic landscapes as input environments  
- Linguistic mediation as transformation process  
- Multimodality as output strategy  
- AR as contextual mediation layer  
- OER principles as design constraint from the outset
