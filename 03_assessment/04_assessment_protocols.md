# 04 Assessment Protocols — Evaluating Learner Progress

## 🧠 Overview

This document defines the assessment protocols for evaluating learner outputs in the Urban Linguistic Landscape OER system. It outlines the process of evaluating multimodal artefacts through rubric-based assessments.

---

## 🧩 Assessment Protocols Overview

The assessment process is designed to measure both the quality of learner outputs and their alignment with the OER’s pedagogical goals. The protocols include both formative and summative assessment practices.

---

## 🧠 Key Assessment Phases

| **Assessment Phase**    | **Description**                                                                                                                   | **Evaluation Criteria**                                                                                                                                                                       |
|-------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Initial Observation**  | Initial engagement with urban linguistic landscapes, followed by documentation and first interpretation of signs and messages.    | Linguistic clarity, relevance of selected content, preliminary mediation, and interpretation.                                                                                                  |
| **Mediation & Transformation** | Learners reinterpret and mediate urban input across languages and cultural contexts.                                        | Linguistic mediation (clarity, cultural context), contextual adaptation, and relevance.                                                                                                       |
| **Multimodal Production** | Creation of multimodal outputs (text, images, audio, video) based on urban linguistic observations.                             | Quality of integration of modes (text, image, audio), coherence, and contextual adequacy.                                                                                                     |
| **Reflection & Evaluation** | Assessment of learner outputs through established rubrics, with a focus on quality, coherence, and pedagogical value.             | Final assessment using rubric-based evaluation across linguistic, intercultural, multimodal dimensions, and OER potential.                                                                     |

---

## 🧠 Assessment Rubrics

Refer to the **Multimodal Rubrics** for detailed evaluation criteria and descriptors at different levels of proficiency (from initial to advanced).

---

## 🧩 Best Practices for Teachers

- **Formative Feedback**: Provide ongoing feedback throughout the assessment process to guide learner improvement.
- **Peer Review**: Encourage peer-to-peer assessment to promote collaborative learning and feedback.
- **Contextual Adaptation**: Customize rubrics to better fit the language level, cultural context, and specific learning goals of your learners.
