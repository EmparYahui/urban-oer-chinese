# Multimodal Urban Trail

## Overview

The Multimodal Urban Trail is a learner-generated digital resource created during a series of Jane’s Walk-inspired explorations of the urban linguistic landscape.

Using Google Maps as a collaborative platform, students documented linguistic, cultural, and spatial features of the city through geolocated multimodal entries. The resulting artefact combines photographs, Chinese-language productions, location-based narratives, and cultural observations within an interactive environment.

This resource illustrates how urban spaces can become learning environments and how learners can act as producers of educational content rather than consumers of pre-existing materials.

## Learning Context

* Language: Chinese as a Foreign Language
* Proficiency level: A1
* Pedagogical approach: Task-Based Language Teaching
* Learning environment: Urban Linguistic Landscapes
* Digital platform: Google Maps

## Learning Objectives

* Observe and interpret elements of the urban linguistic landscape.
* Connect language learning with authentic urban environments.
* Develop mediation skills through contextual explanation.
* Create multimodal educational resources.
* Contribute to open and shareable learning materials.
