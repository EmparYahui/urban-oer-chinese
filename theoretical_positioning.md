# Theoretical Positioning — Urban Linguistic Landscape OER

This project is situated at the intersection of task-based language teaching (TBLT), linguistic landscape studies, and multimodal pedagogies in second language acquisition. It conceptualises urban space as a semiotic and pedagogical environment where learners engage in situated meaning-making through observation, mediation, and transformation of real-world linguistic input.

The framework extends traditional TBLT by integrating multimodal artefact production and open educational resource (OER) design principles, enabling learners to generate reusable educational outputs rather than task-bound performance artefacts. In doing so, it aligns with recent work in multimodal literacy, translanguaging pedagogy, and spatialised language learning.

Furthermore, the inclusion of augmented reality (AR) and geolocation components positions the project within emerging research on embodied and spatial language learning, where digital and physical environments converge to support experiential mediation processes.

Overall, the model proposes a shift from task completion to knowledge production, reframing language learning as a process of open, situated, and reusable meaning-making.
