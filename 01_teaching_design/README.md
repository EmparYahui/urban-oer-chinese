# Teaching Design — Urban Linguistic Landscape OER (A1–A2 Chinese)

## Overview

This folder contains the pedagogical design for a task-based language learning intervention in beginner-level Chinese (A1–A2 CEFR). The design is grounded in urban linguistic landscapes, multimodal learning, and open educational practices (OER).

The instructional model integrates task-based language teaching (TBLT), linguistic mediation, multimodal production, and augmented reality (AR) as a contextual mediational layer.

---

## Pedagogical principles

- Task-Based Language Teaching (TBLT) as the core instructional approach  
- Urban linguistic landscapes as authentic semiotic input spaces  
- Mediation as a central mechanism for meaning-making  
- Multimodal production (text, image, optional audio/video)  
- Hybrid writing combining analog and digital processes  
- Openness as a design principle embedded from the outset  
- AR as a spatial and contextual mediational layer  

---

## Learning objectives

By the end of the module, learners will be able to:

- Identify and interpret linguistic landscape elements in urban environments  
- Mediate meaning between source and target language in situated contexts  
- Produce multimodal artefacts combining linguistic and visual elements  
- Develop basic communicative competence in Chinese (A1–A2 level)  
- Adapt language output for audience awareness and reuse potential  
- Contribute to Open Educational Resources (OER) through their productions  

---

## Instructional sequence

### Phase 1 — Urban exploration
Learners observe and document linguistic landscape elements such as signs, shop names, advertisements, and graffiti in real urban environments.

### Phase 2 — Multimodal production
Learners create digital artefacts combining text and image (and optionally audio/video), recontextualising urban input into educational content.

### Phase 3 — AR integration
Learner-generated artefacts are linked to physical locations using augmented reality tools, enabling situated access to content.

---

## Role of OER

Open Educational Resources are not treated as final outputs but as a design principle embedded throughout the learning process.

This implies that:

- Tasks are designed for reuse and adaptation from the outset  
- Learner outputs are treated as potential educational resources  
- Pedagogical clarity and transferability are core design criteria  
- Outputs may be published under CC BY-SA 4.0 licence  

---

## Reusability

This teaching design can be:

- adapted for other languages and proficiency levels  
- recontextualised in different urban environments  
- integrated into task-based, project-based, or CLIL frameworks  
- combined with alternative digital or AR platforms
