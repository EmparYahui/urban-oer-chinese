# 02 Design Principles — Urban Linguistic Landscape OER

## 🧠 Core principle

All instructional design is based on a single integrated logic:

> Urban space functions as a semiotic learning environment where tasks generate reusable educational artefacts.

---

## 🧩 Design principles

### 1. Situated learning principle
Learning must emerge from real urban linguistic environments (ULL).

### 2. Task authenticity principle
Tasks must simulate or reproduce real communicative and interpretive practices.

### 3. Mediation principle
All learning involves transformation of meaning across languages, modes, or contexts.

### 4. Multimodality principle
Learner output must integrate at least two semiotic modes (e.g., text + image).

### 5. OER-by-design principle
Every task is designed with potential reuse and openness in mind.

### 6. AR contextualization principle
Augmented Reality is used as a spatial and interpretive extension layer, not as decoration.

---

## 🧭 Design constraints

- Tasks must be scalable from A1 to A2
- Tasks must allow observable learner output
- Tasks must produce reusable artefacts
- Tasks must connect to assessment criteria

---

- Pedagogical rules for task creation
- Structural constraints for learning design
