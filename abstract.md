# Abstract

This paper presents the Urban Linguistic Landscape OER, a pedagogical framework for task- and project-based Chinese language learning (A1–A2) that integrates urban linguistic landscapes, multimodal mediation, and open educational resource design.

The system reconfigures traditional task-based language teaching by positioning urban space as a site of situated meaning-making, where learners engage in observation, mediation, and transformation of real-world linguistic input. Through a structured sequence of learning tasks, multimodal artefact production, and assessment frameworks, learners generate reusable educational outputs rather than task-bound performance artifacts.

The design incorporates principles from linguistic landscape studies, multimodal pedagogy, translanguaging theory, and design-based research, while extending them through the integration of optional augmented reality (AR) and geolocation-based learning components.

Findings from the implementation suggest that the model supports increased learner engagement in mediation processes, enhanced multimodal awareness, and the production of open, reusable learning artefacts. The framework also demonstrates potential for scalability and adaptation across languages, contexts, and educational settings.

Overall, the project proposes a shift from task completion to knowledge production within language learning environments, positioning learners as active contributors to open educational ecosystems.
